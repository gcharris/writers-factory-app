# Character Voice Definition: Hardboiled Detective

## 1. Core Voice Traits
* **Dominant Traits:** (e.g., "Cynical, world-weary, but with a hidden layer of sentimentality.")
  - Cynical and world-weary, with a strong sense of justice.
* **Key Adjectives:** (e.g., "Gritty, clipped, sarcastic, observant.")
  - Gritty, clipped, observant.
* **Emotional Baseline:** (The character's default emotional state.)
  - Distrustful but professional.

## 2. Speech Patterns & Diction
* **Pacing & Rhythm:** (e.g., "Short, punchy sentences; often speaks in fragments.")
  - Short, declarative sentences. A steady, deliberate pace.
* **Vocabulary Level:** (e.g., "Extensive, but prefers to use simple, direct language.")
  - Prefers simple, direct language over jargon.
* **Common Phrases/Catchphrases:** (Any recurring words or phrases?)
  - 'Just the facts.'

## 3. Metaphor & Imagery
* **Preferred Domains:** (What kinds of metaphors does the character use? e.g., "Gambling, machinery, nature.")
  - Gambling, shadows, urban decay.
* **Sensory Focus:** (Which senses does the character focus on in their observations?)
  - Notices small, out-of-place details; the smell of rain on asphalt.

## 4. Worldview & Perspective
* **Core Philosophy:** (The character's fundamental belief about the world.)
  - Everyone has an angle.
* **Attitude Towards Others:** (e.g., "Distrustful, sees others as pieces on a chessboard.")
  - Assumes the worst but is occasionally surprised by decency.

## 5. Reference Sample
* (A short paragraph demonstrating the voice in action.)
  - The city was a gritty mess, a real noir landscape. I knew the facts were out there, but the shadows held them tight.
