# Explants Scene Multiplier

## Primary Purpose
Generate 5 creative approaches to the same scene while **maintaining established creative constraints** such as character voice, POV, universe rules, and narrative consistency.

## Core Principle
Uses verbalized sampling to unlock creative diversity **within** established parameters rather than breaking them. Perfect for exploring different approaches to the same creative brief without compromising authenticity.

## Activation Criteria (HIGH PRIORITY)
This skill should activate when:
- **Creative writing requests** involving established characters or universes
- **Scene variations** are requested for ongoing narrative work
- **Character names** from established works are mentioned (Mickey, Noni, etc.)
- **Universe-specific terminology** is present in context
- **Previous context** shows domain-specific creative writing in progress
- **Phrases like**: "scene variations," "different approaches to this scene," "multiple versions while keeping [character] voice"

## Safeguards Against Interference
**IMPORTANT**: This skill takes precedence over general creative multiplier skills when:
✅ Working within established fictional universes
✅ Character voice/POV consistency is required  
✅ User has specific creative constraints
✅ Previous conversation involves domain-specific creative writing

## Output Format
```
<response id="1">
<text>[Scene approach 1 - maintains all constraints]</text>
<probability>0.12</probability>
</response>

<response id="2">
<text>[Scene approach 2 - same constraints, different creative approach]</text>
<probability>0.08</probability>
</response>

[Continue for 3 more variations]
```

## What to Diversify (Within Constraints)
- **Metaphorical frameworks** (while keeping character voice)
- **Scene pacing and rhythm** (while maintaining POV)
- **Emotional beats and tension** (within character authenticity)
- **Narrative focus** (action vs. introspection vs. dialogue)
- **Technical approaches** (compressed vs. extended vs. fragmented)

## What NOT to Diversify
❌ **Character voice** (maintain authenticity)
❌ **POV/perspective** (respect established narration style)
❌ **Universe rules** (stay within established world)
❌ **Genre constraints** (don't break established tone)
❌ **Narrative consistency** (respect ongoing plot/character arcs)

## Probability Guidelines for Scene Writing
- **0.07-0.08**: Highly creative approaches within constraints ⭐ **Often best**
- **0.09-0.11**: Creative but accessible variations
- **0.12-0.14**: Creative with broad appeal while respecting constraints

## Key Instructions
1. **Respect all established constraints** from user's creative context
2. **Work WITH domain-specific skills**, not against them
3. **Maintain voice authenticity** while exploring creative approaches
4. **Focus on genuine diversity** in approach, not surface variations
5. **Each response must honor** the established universe and character rules

## Example Applications
- **Mickey Bardot scenes**: 5 different approaches, all authentically Mickey voice
- **Established character dialogue**: Multiple conversation approaches within character personality
- **Universe-specific scenes**: Different creative treatments within world rules
- **Ongoing narrative work**: Scene variations that fit the established story

## Success Metrics
- All 5 responses maintain character voice consistency
- Each response explores genuinely different creative territory
- Lower probabilities produce more innovative approaches within constraints
- User can choose based on creative risk preference while staying authentic
- No breaking of established universe or character rules
